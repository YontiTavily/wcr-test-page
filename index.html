Welcome to this test page. The banana is one of the most widely consumed fruits in the world, grown in tropical regions across Southeast Asia, Latin America, and Africa. It belongs to the genus Musa and is technically classified as a berry by botanical standards.

Bananas are rich in potassium, vitamin B6, and dietary fiber. They serve as a staple food in many developing countries and are exported globally, with Ecuador, the Philippines, and Costa Rica being among the top producers.

The Cavendish variety dominates international trade, though it faces significant threats from Panama disease Tropical Race 4, a fungal pathogen that has spread across plantations worldwide. Researchers are actively developing resistant cultivars to ensure future supply stability.

Beyond nutrition, banana plants have diverse uses. Their leaves serve as natural plates and food wrappers in many cultures. The fibers extracted from banana pseudostems are used in textile production, particularly in the Philippines where banana fiber fabric has a long tradition.

In culinary applications, bananas appear in everything from smoothies and baked goods to savory dishes like plantain chips and banana curry. The fruits natural sweetness and creamy texture make it a versatile ingredient across cuisines.

Global banana trade is valued at over twelve billion dollars annually, making it one of the most important fruit crops economically. The industry employs millions of workers across the supply chain from plantation to retail.
